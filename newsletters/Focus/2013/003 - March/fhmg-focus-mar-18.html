Clearing Up the Confusion on Medication Samples

How medications are used and given to patients is a very important topic, especially when it comes to legal terminology as stated in Florida statutes and subject to review by the Department of Health (DOH) inspectors. Take a moment to brush up on your knowledge so you’ll be ready the next time a DOH inspector comes knocking.

Dispensing: This occurs when medication is given to a patient and there is payment for the prescription. For a physician in the State of Florida to dispense medications he/she must have a Dispensing Practitioner designation attached to their license. Additionally, the physician must physically write a prescription and offer it to the patient to take elsewhere or allow the prescription to be filled by the practice.

Administering Medications: When a physician or practice MA/nurse actually treats a patient in the office with a medication, this is called administering medications. There is no dispensing of medications taking place, just removal from cabinet or refrigerator prior to administering.

Distributing Samples: Since there is no payment by the patient, medication samples that are given to patients are not considered dispensed. The samples are considered given or distributed to patients to take home.
