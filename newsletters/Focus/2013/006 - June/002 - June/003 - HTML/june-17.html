New Physicians Join FHMG
Please welcome our newest physician as FHMG continues to grow and broaden its medical presence in Central Florida.

Delos Clift, MD, FACS, RPVI
Vascular Institute of Central Florida

Delos Clift, MD, FACS, RPVI is a board-certified surgeon, fellowship-trained in peripheral vascular surgery and a registered physician in vascular interpretation. Dr. Clift joined Vascular Institute of Central Florida in June of 2013. Throughout his career, Dr. Clift has delivered groundbreaking vascular and endovascular surgical care at a variety of private practice and hospital settings in Michigan and Florida. He has been affiliated with Florida Hospital since 1991 and has served on numerous committees and played integral roles, including surgical director of the dialysis unit and instructor of vascular technology for the Florida Hospital School of cardiovascular Diagnostics. 407.303.7250
 


CFO Joins FHMG Leadership Team

Mike Mazeres
Chief Financial Officer

With the continued growth of FHMG, it is critical to continue strengthening our financial leadership and expertise. Therefore, we are pleased to announce that Mike Mazeres, CPA, CMPE has assumed the role of Chief Financial Officer (CFO) for FHMG. A seasoned financial executive, Mike has more than 20 years of experience in physician practice management and financial leadership. Most recently, Mike served as CFO/COO at Select Physician’s Alliance, P.L. in Tampa, FL. He has also served as Executive Director at SMH Radiology Associates, P.A. in Sarasota, FL. Please join us in welcoming Mike to the team.

Spiritual Ambassadors: Reflecting Your Excellence in Care

You might not know it, but a Spiritual Ambassador may be closer than you think. They could be a nurse, an office manager, a practice director or even a physician. In addition to their daily work, these emissaries are ambassadors of our culture, ideals and mission.

[Can we make this a callout box, grey background or something to highlight quote.] “Becoming a Spiritual Ambassador was just a natural step for me ‘to extend the healing ministry of Christ.’” – Jason Salagubang, MD, FHMG Physician

To illustrate just some of the things our emissaries do, two of our Spiritual Ambassadors, Carol and Christine from Florida Heart Group, recently shared the following story with us.

“During our Spiritual Ambassador meeting we talked about mission and what it means to extend “His” hands to those in need. Carol told of a long-time patient who she had been working with. One particular day he was cross and snapped at her no matter how patiently she responded. Finally Carol laid her hand on his arm and said, "OK, what is going on? You usually aren't like this. What is wrong?" His eyes filled with tears as he told her that he just lost his son and he had been diagnosed with cancer. He was very angry at life. Carol prayed with him and his whole demeanor changed, a peace came over him. At that moment, prayer and a caring heart calmed the storm raging inside of him.

This story from Carol and Christine is a simple reminder to all of us to look beyond the actions of others with understanding, treating with compassion and caring for both physical and emotional needs.

The Goals of Our Spiritual Ambassadors

  • To transform our work environment into a sanctuary where trust between coworkers, physicians and volunteers is experienced through authentic relationships, one encounter at a time.
  • To create and nurture a sense of family through the ministry of service, prayer and department activities that bring hope to the hearts of all.
  • To represent Christ in the way we care.
If you are interested in learning more about becoming a Spiritual Ambassador, please contact Stephanie.Whitley@flhosp.org.
